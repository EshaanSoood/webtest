We're off to the races

We had our first couple of rehearsals for the album last week. Since all this is so new to me I completely forgot to turn the camera on in the storm that is leading a band. On this album, 2 of the songs are older songs I wrote back in 2019, 1 is a song I wrote last year, and one earlier this year. These are the four songs that the band has played quite a bit at this point, however there are also four new songs that I have written over the last few weeks and it is always a fun challenge to put these new songs together. Unlike when I write pop music, where there is a specific idea for what the song should sound like, in jazz I try to not give a mountain of information to the musicians. This is so that they're musical identities can shine through while still keeping the composition in line with what I envision it being. All this to say, while doing all of that I forgot to take Behind The Scenes footage of the rehearsal, but l've learned my lesson. Yester day, on 26th March we also had the chance to play this music along with some songs that are not making the record live at a local venue. I did remember to turn the camera on for this one so there is some of that available here
Miss Lightning

This song is dedicated to the being that taught me what love is. and what love can be - my
dearest Bijli. Bijli was a tan cocker spaniel that came into my life a few months after I went blind. She really turned a lot of people who were scared of dogs into dog lovers or at least Bijli lovers. Bijli unfortunately passed away this January and all of it was especially hard being in a different country, far away. Music is one of the only few ways I know of how to deal with things so I wrote this song, Miss Lightning because the word Bijli translates to Lightning and she was definitely a little Missy and I also will always deeply Miss her. The song starts of
rather sombre, but then soon picks up and becomes more and more cheerful, this was because I didn’t want to really remember her by the fact that I was grieving but rather all the cute and energetic things she did. to brighten our life. After you hear the main theme twice, you hear my friend Misha Studenkov take a beautiful piano solo followed by a guitar solo. This solo part is based on some of the best parts of the main theme, just like how we remember all the greatest parts about someone when they pass away. However grief does have an interesting way to catch up with you when you least expect it, I tried to represent that with the part where all the instruments except the guitar and drums drop out. But then I eventually did get over my grief, which is represented in the return of the theme.
I hope you enjoy this song and having a small little piece of Bijli with you.
