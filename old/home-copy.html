Hello! If you are here, you might already know who I am, if not my name is Eshaan Sood. I’m a musician from New Delhi, India. This page is here for the people who would like to support my endeavour to record and release my debut instrumental music album. My band is called the Sonic Alchemists, which I have put together in multiple configurations over the last 6 years.

Over this time period I have written a lot of music and worked extensively on my craft, a pursuit that led me to the Eastman School of Music where I study Jazz Performance. In 2023 I found some amazing musical collaborators here in New York and we have been playing together ever since. We’re finally at the stage where we can get into the studio and record some of this beautiful music.

the band

The Sonic Alchemists is a group I’ve put together over the years in many diﬀerent configurations to merge my love for a lot of varied musical styles. I finally have the dream team of musicians. This debut album is going to have 8 songs.

Each song is based on a story or experience that I’ve encountered over the last 7 years travelling through music. This campaign aims to allow for the project to come alive through a multimedia medium. The music and videos for the stories that go with them.

what is crowdfunding and why use it?

Like any other business, the arts require funding. Putting together a project of this scope and quality requires a lot of costs that streaming music on Spotify cannot cover. To give you an example, I would need about 2 million streams on Spotify to fund this project. Crowdfunding is a means to gather supporters who are willing to be patrons of this project and help bring it to life.I have never really put out this kind of call for support for my music in the past but as I begin this exciting new chapter in my life as an artist I want to bring you, the listener along for the ride. There are exciting perks that you can partake in if you so choose.

more questions?

What is the timeline of the project?

Currently we are booked to record the album on April 26th and 27th. Once the album is recorded, the final takes will be selected by May 3rd. These takes will then be sent to the mix engineer, this process can take up to 4-6 weeks. Taking the longer time frame as a measure, by June 20th the album will be sent for Mastering and by the 1st of July the album will be ready. Between May 3and July 1 all the artwork, videos and promo material will be created. If all goes as per plan, byJuly 15th the album should be announced for a mid August release.

Where is the album being recorded?

The album will be recorded at Bunker Studios on April 26th and 27th. Bunker Studios is a world class recording studio with some of the best engineers on the planet.

Who is playing on the album?

The band on the album is Annie Orzen on Piano, Ivan Demarjian On Saxophone, and Sam Schafferon on Drums. And of course, me on guitar.

Is all the music ready? Can I hear some of it?

Yes we have more music than we can record in two days. Even then I am continuing to write till the day we hit the studio in case something magical appears and we can capture it. As for what we already have, there is some music available from live performances on youtube. Additionally, people in the Fan Tier will get regular updates from rehearsals and events that will show case some of the music.

What are these video stories you mention in the breakdown?

All the songs are based on stories, for example one of my Songs ‘A Plea For Forgiveness.’ was written when a lot of things were going wrong in my life and I found myself asking the universe to forgive me for whatever I did to annoy it. After a while of feeling miserable, I realised that things can go bad on their own accord but rarely do they magically get great. To make things great you have to get your hands dirty and work on things and stop feeling sorry for yourself. The song has three sections that represent these ideas. The videos will share these stories

Can’t playing shows recuperate these costs?

Touring and playing shows is a volatile industry. Even then, playing shows requires its own separate investment that is not part of this project. In fact your support with this recording will help to propel the live show side of this project as well. So in a way, it is the album that leads to the shows and not the other way around.

What is mixing and mastering and why does it cost so much?

Once audio has been recorded it goes through the process of mixing . This process takes all the raw audio, balances the levels, adjusts the frequencies so that instruments don’t clash with each other and in general make the recording sound cohesive and like if the band was in a room playing for you. After mixing is complete the recording is sent for mastering, which ensures that the songs play at a good volume on any sound system, that there is not too much extra bass or too little volume range.

I heard people can record in their houses, why does this project cost so much?

While recording at home is possible, recording five musicians live in a house actually requires equipment that costs more than this project. More over you need expertise with recording, mixing and mastering. These skills take years to develop and this cost of the project is currently at its bare minimum to achieve a professional quality recording and release.

What will you do if you exceed your goal?

If we exceed our goal, it will enable us to do quite a few things. First off, we would be able to extend our promotional budget to get this music across to many more people. If we do exceed our goal, depending on the amount we exceed it by, we will be able to enable things like Vinyl Production, Live shows potentially in your city, hiring a professional photographer for the behind the scenes photographs, other merchandise items like T-shirts and hoodies, and maybe even recording more than 8 songs.
